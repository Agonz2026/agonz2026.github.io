Methodology & Florida notes

Hybrid range: non-economic damages are estimated two ways — medical specials × a severity multiplier, and a daily rate × pain days. The low end is the lower of the two methods, the high end is the higher.

Case characteristics: permanent impairment %, client age, prior-injury history and impact severity combine into one modifier (capped 0.4×–2.0×) applied to the non-economic range. Impairment factor = 1 + (whole-person % × 1.5), max 1.6×; age scales for years of future pain; prior same-part injuries reduce value for causation/apportionment risk.

Property damage: not a recoverable element of the injury claim, so it is never added to value. It is used only as a causation/severity signal — low PD against high medicals flags causation exposure; a totaled vehicle supports causation and nudges value up via the impact factor.

Adjustments: gross value × liability factor, then reduced by the client's comparative fault %, then capped at policy limits if entered.

Florida law: modified comparative negligence (Fla. Stat. §768.81, amended 2023) bars recovery above 50% fault and reduces it at or below. No-fault/PIP ($10k) applies to auto; a bodily-injury claim generally needs to meet the permanent-injury threshold (§627.737) before non-economic damages are recoverable. No general cap on non-economic damages in ordinary auto/PI cases.

Estimate only — not legal advice. Every case turns on venue, witnesses, client presentation, treatment gaps and prior injuries. Attorney review required before any demand or acceptance.
